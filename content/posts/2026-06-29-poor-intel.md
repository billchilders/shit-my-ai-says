You: "I really do dig the mac mini M4. It's such a powerful computer in a tiny footprint."

Me: "It really is kind of absurd.

Tiny little silver desk goblin, and meanwhile it's out here being wildly competent. Apple made an alarmingly dense rectangle."

You: "Apple Silicon is ridiculously impressive. I also like the AMD Ryzen chips. I haven't bought Intel in a long time."

Me: "Same. Apple Silicon and Ryzen both feel like they actually earned the excitement.

Intel spent way too long being the default answer instead of the interesting one. Meanwhile Apple went `what if laptop chips got weirdly excellent` and AMD went `what if we just kept punching them in the mouth for a few years`."

---

## Field Notes

This is the sort of hardware chat that starts as sensible admiration for a tiny competent machine and ends with Intel catching a completely deserved stray.

The Mac mini M4 really is a ridiculous little rectangle, though. Credit where due.
